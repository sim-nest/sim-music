# sim-lib-pitch-namer-jazz

In one line: Reads and recognises jazz chord symbols, turning names like Cmaj7 or Am7/G into chords and back.

## What it gives you

This implements the jazz naming school. It parses jazz chord symbols such as Cmaj7 or Am7 over a G bass into a structured symbol, realises them as concrete chords, and works the other way too: given a group of notes it recognises which jazz chord they form by trying every root and quality. It speaks the shorthand working jazz musicians actually write on a chart.

## Why you will be glad

- Turn a written jazz symbol into the notes it names.
- Recognise the jazz chord hiding in a group of pitches.
- Handle slash chords with a named bass note.

## Where it fits

This is the jazz voice in the SIM pitch-naming family. It joins the naming aggregator beside the Forte, roman, and Riemannian schools, letting the constellation label and understand harmony the way lead sheets and jazz charts express it.
