# sim-lib-sound-timbre

In one line: A library of instrument voices and the filters that colour them, from pure sines to plucked strings and bells.

## What it gives you

This defines timbre -- a named synthesis recipe with a default envelope, descriptive metadata, and a chain of filters. Its recipes cover pure sine, sawtooth, square, and triangle waves, organ pipe, plucked Karplus-Strong strings, FM, and inharmonic bell sounds, and its filter family shapes their spectra. A runtime surface installs the built-in timbre cards as a library, so a set of ready voices is available to play the moment you need them.

## Why you will be glad

- Pick from ready voices spanning classic and physical models.
- Colour any voice with a chain of spectral filters.
- Reach a bench of built-in timbres already registered for use.

## Where it fits

This is the voice library of the SIM sound family. The sound bridge draws on these timbres to give MIDI programs a sound, and the General MIDI catalogue maps standard program numbers onto them, so the constellation has a shared palette of instrument colours.
